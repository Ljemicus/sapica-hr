# PetPark blog – recommended next phases

## What phase 1 covered
- Stronger category-based CTAs on article pages
- Better related/internal link paths from article pages
- Clearer article metadata (published + updated)
- Improved featured hero treatment for blog articles

## Recommended next phases

### Phase 2 — category hubs (highest priority)
**Why:** biggest SEO + UX gain after article-page polish.

Build category landing pages that aggregate:
- intro copy for the category intent
- featured / newest articles
- evergreen guides
- relevant PetPark service links
- FAQ snippets for the category

Suggested first hubs:
1. `Zdravlje ljubimaca`
2. `Prehrana pasa`
3. `Školovanje pasa`
4. `Život sa psom u gradu`

## Phase 3 — FAQ schema + article schema enrichment
**Why:** low build cost, good organic search upside.

Add:
- optional per-article FAQ blocks in content data
- JSON-LD FAQ schema only when real FAQ content exists
- richer BlogPosting fields (image, dateModified, about, keywords)

Do **not** auto-generate fake FAQ blocks. Only add where editorially justified.

## Phase 4 — local SEO article plan
**Why:** PetPark already has strong local/service intent to connect with editorial traffic.

Create a focused local content cluster around:
- Rijeka
- Zagreb
- Split

Suggested article types:
- city pet-owner guides
- “best dog-friendly places in [city]”
- “how to find a sitter/trainer/groomer in [city]”
- seasonal local guides (summer heat, winter rain, flea/tick season)

Important: map each article to a commercial next step, not just traffic.

## Phase 5 — email capture / retention
**Why:** blog traffic should not bounce forever into the void like a dramatic cat.

Best first version:
- lightweight newsletter/signup block on article pages
- category-aware lead magnet or subscription copy
- optional exit or end-of-article capture only after baseline inline form exists

Keep it simple first:
- one inline block
- one thank-you state
- one list / audience segment by topic interest

## Nice-to-have later
- manually curated “recommended reading path” sequences
- article thumbnails/images from a proper content field or CMS
- author profiles with dedicated author pages
- article tags and better semantic related-post logic
- editorial QA pass on older article copy quality / formatting

## Suggested implementation order
1. Category hubs
2. FAQ schema with real FAQ content
3. Local SEO content cluster
4. Email capture
5. Richer author/content architecture
